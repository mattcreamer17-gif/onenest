the picture

Four product surfaces, one shared operating team, one audit trail that has to hold up on paper.

OneNest runs four product surfaces (HealthBird, MedPass, CareBird, TravelBird) on a shared operating team out of Miami. HealthBird is the revenue core, with 1,500+ paying members and 90+ carrier contracts across 25+ states. MedPass is the recurring wellness engine, with a 24/7 LLM chat seeded by clinicians and the Aiberry partnership layered in for mental-health screening. CareBird and TravelBird stretch the footprint into hospital EHR and travel insurance.

The Ecuador IESS engagement is where the portfolio ran into a question it could not answer fast enough on paper: what is the audit trail, where is the governance documented, and who is accountable for each AI-driven decision.

the eduba frame

Most of what a holding company of this shape needs is not another model. It is a shared context layer the four brands can inherit from.

Privacy policy, AI behavior, consent flows, and incident-response procedures live in one versioned place and resolve into each brand without copy-paste drift. Here is how we typically see the work break down.

60%

Traditional code and database hygiene

Uniform policy storage, versioned documents, per-brand overlays, inspection tooling.

30%

Rule-based governance

Role gates, regional compliance variants, carrier-specific business rules.

10%

Genuine LLM work

Concentrated in MedPass's chat and the clinical notetaking surface.

The value sits in getting the 90 percent right first.
